Mom's Recipe Book

Even before dad made the box, there were the recipe cards. Most were written in Mom’s handwriting, but a few were contributed by friends. There are even a couple in dad’s handwriting. The favorites, often bearing stains accumulated over the years, came along in a zip lock on adventures to Germany and New Zealand.

Then there were the books, written to share both the recipes and family stories associated with them to a wider circle of friends and family. Those, too, now bear the stains that come from years of use. No more hunting for the cards. We all know that biscuits are #9 in The Joy of Breakfast for Normal People (and for my family and friends, too).

And now there is a website. We hope it doesn’t lead to stained electronic devices — you have been warned — but brings just as much joy as tastes, smells, and stories are shared with family and friends.
